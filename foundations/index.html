How It Works

Not lessons.
A development system.

Most pitching instruction is reactive — a coach watches your kid throw, gives some tips, and you come back next week hoping something stuck. There's often no structure connecting one session to the next.

Foundations works differently. Every session is part of a defined progression. Skills are introduced, reinforced, then tested before advancing. Your pitcher doesn't move forward by showing up — they move forward by demonstrating they're ready.

1

Assessment

Every pitcher begins with a 90-minute in-person evaluation. No one skips this step — it's how we understand where your pitcher actually is.

2

Placement

Based on the assessment, your pitcher is placed into the right phase and subphase. Training starts where it should — not too early, not too late.

3

Reserved Weekly Training

Training times are assigned in advance. No weekly booking, no scheduling friction — just show up and develop.

4

Advance When Ready

No pitcher moves to the next phase just by attending. Advancement is earned by demonstrating the ability to perform and repeat what's being trained.

5

60/10 Club

The destination. 60%+ strikes across two consecutive competitive bullpens and a +10 MPH fastball gain since starting.

Common Questions

Before you schedule.

How is this different from private lessons?

Many athletes benefit from both — but they solve different problems. Lessons are adjustments. Foundations is development.

Traditional Lessons

Does my pitcher need experience to start?

No. Foundations is built for any pitcher age 8 and up — whether they're brand new to pitching, struggling with consistency, or have had instruction before without consistent results. The assessment determines where your pitcher starts.

How often does my pitcher need to train?

There's no required weekly count — pitchers train at a pace that fits their schedule. That said, pitchers who average 2 sessions per week typically complete one subphase per month. Up to 8 sessions per month are available.

What happens at the assessment?

A 90-minute in-person evaluation. Free, no commitment to enroll. Parent and pitcher both attend.

Athlete Background

Training history, experience, season status — scored to determine starting phase.

Pitch Repertoire & Game Knowledge

What they throw, how long, and a brief game knowledge check.

Warm-Up Quality Grading

Every warm-up movement graded 1–5. Re-graded at future sessions to track progress.

Athleticism Benchmarks

Broad jump, single-leg balance, rotational med ball throw, 10-yard acceleration.

Movement Observation

8–12 throws on flat ground to identify the dominant pattern and first session.

Documented Output

A documented start profile specific to your pitcher.
